The **Activity Logs** provide a complete, chronological history of everything that happens in your Forest Admin project. Every record edit, action triggered, note left, and administrative change is captured and stored.

This is a critical tool for teams that require **accountability**, **compliance**, and **troubleshooting** capabilities.

## Accessing activity logs

Navigate to the **Activity Logs** tab from the main navigation. You'll see a timeline of events across the entire project.

Each entry in the activity log shows:

- **Timestamp** — when the event occurred.
- **User** — which operator or admin performed the action.
- **Event type** — what kind of activity was recorded (edit, action, note, approval, etc.).
- **Target** — the record or resource affected.
- **Details** — the specific change made (e.g. field name, old value, new value).

## What gets logged

The Activity Logs capture:

| Event type | Examples |
|------------|---------|
| **Record edits** | A field value was changed on a record |
| **Smart Actions** | An operator triggered an action (with or without approval) |
| **Approval Workflow** | A request was created, approved, or rejected |
| **Notes** | A note was created or updated on a record |
| **Inbox events** | A task was assigned, started, completed, or unassigned |
| **Administrative changes** | Role or permission changes, user added/removed |

## Filtering the activity log

Use the **filter bar** at the top to narrow down the log:

- Filter by **user** to see all actions taken by a specific team member.
- Filter by **event type** to focus on edits, actions, or approvals.
- Filter by **date range** to review a specific period.
- Filter by **collection or record** to see everything that happened to a specific piece of data.

## Use cases

**Accountability:** Understand who changed what and when — invaluable for post-incident reviews or compliance audits.

**Troubleshooting:** Trace back a sequence of events that led to an unexpected data state.

**Compliance:** Demonstrate that data access and modifications follow your organization's policies.

**Team oversight:** Monitor team activity patterns and identify unusually high or low activity.
