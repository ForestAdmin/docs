**Escalation Workflows** let you define rules that automatically escalate records when they've been waiting too long, haven't been processed, or require urgent attention. This helps teams manage SLAs and ensure nothing gets stuck in a queue.

## What is Escalation?

Escalation is the automatic process of raising the priority of a record or notifying a supervisor when a defined condition is met — typically a time-based or status-based threshold.

**Common escalation triggers:**
- A support ticket has not been assigned within 2 hours.
- A document has been in the "Waiting for Validation" state for more than 24 hours.
- A high-priority order has not been fulfilled by its deadline.

## Escalation Actions

When an escalation rule is triggered, one or more of the following actions can occur:

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Notify a team lead** | Send an alert to a supervisor or specific user |
| **Reassign the record** | Move the task to a different operator or team |
| **Change priority** | Update a field to indicate elevated urgency |
| **Trigger a Smart Action** | Execute custom logic defined by your developers |

## Use Cases

**Customer support:** Escalate tickets that haven't received a first response within your SLA window.

**Document review:** Flag documents that have been in a review queue longer than allowed by compliance rules.

**Incident management:** Alert the on-call team lead when an incident has been open for more than a defined threshold.

**Approval delays:** Escalate approval requests that have been pending for too long.

## Setting Up Escalation Rules

Escalation rules are configured in your project settings. Each rule defines:

1. **The trigger condition** — which segment or field state activates the rule.
2. **The time threshold** — how long the condition must be true before escalating.
3. **The escalation action** — what happens when the rule fires.

Contact your Forest Admin admin or account manager to configure escalation rules for your project.

## Monitoring

Escalation events are recorded in the **[Activity Logs](/product/manage/activity)**, giving you a full audit trail of when and why escalations occurred.
