Forest generates a default layout from your database schema. This step is about making it useful for the people who'll work in it every day.

## Organize your collections and fields

Open the **Layout Editor** (the pencil icon in the top bar) to enter edit mode. From there you can:

- **Rename** collections and fields to match your business vocabulary (e.g. `users` → `Customers`)
- **Reorder** fields in the detail view to surface the most important information first
- **Hide** fields that are irrelevant for your team's daily work

Changes in the Layout Editor are saved as configuration — they don't affect your database schema.

<Info>
The Layout Editor is only available to users with the **Admin** role. [Learn more about roles](/get-started/control/roles-permissions).
</Info>

## Create a segment

Segments are saved filters that let your team quickly access specific subsets of data — for example "Pending orders" or "Enterprise customers".

To create a segment:

1. Open a collection
2. Apply the filters you want (e.g. `status = pending`)
3. Click **Save as segment** and give it a name

The segment will appear in the left sidebar for that collection.

<Info>
You can also create **smart segments** with custom backend logic for more complex filtering. See [Segments](/product/process/segments/creating-segments).
</Info>
