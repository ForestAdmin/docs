Your back-office is in production. This step is about giving your team access, with the right level of permissions for each person.

## Invite a user

1. Go to **Project Settings** → **Users**
2. Click **Invite user**
3. Enter their email address and select a role

The user will receive an invitation email and can access the back-office immediately after signing up.

## Understand roles

Forest has three built-in permission levels:

| Role | What they can do |
|---|---|
| **Admin** | Full access, manage settings, layouts, users, and data |
| **Editor** | Edit layouts and configurations, but can't manage users or billing |
| **User** | Access data only, no configuration access |

Roles control access to the Forest interface itself. For granular control over what data each person can see or edit, use **Teams** and **Permissions**.

## Create a team

Teams let you group users and apply specific permissions to the group, for example, a Support team that can only view customer data, or a Finance team that can trigger refund actions.

1. Go to **Project Settings** → **Teams**
2. Click **New team** and give it a name
3. Add users to the team
4. Configure the team's permissions: which collections they can access, which actions they can trigger, which fields are visible

<Info>
For advanced permission setups, scopes, field-level visibility, action restrictions, see [Roles & Permissions](/get-started/control/roles-permissions).
</Info>
